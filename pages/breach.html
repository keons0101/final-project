Breach

2025 | 47:23

Breach is the eighth studio album by American musical duo Twenty One Pilots, released on September 12, 2025, through Fueled by Ramen. It is a follow-up to the duo's previous album Clancy (2024), and concludes a conceptual series also consisting of Blurryface (2015), Trench (2018), and Scaled and Icy (2021). Primarily written by vocalist Tyler Joseph and co-produced with Paul Meany and Mike Elizondo, the album received positive reviews from critics and was a commercial success, becoming the duo's second record to debut atop the Billboard 200, and the biggest opening week vinyl sales for a rock album since tracking began in 1991.

The album features a mix of alternative rock, pop, and electronic elements, showcasing the duo's versatility and willingness to experiment with new sounds. Tracks like "Downstairs" and "Drum Show" highlight the band's ability to craft catchy melodies while addressing serious topics such as anxiety, self-discovery, and resilience.

"Breach" has been praised for its cohesive narrative and production quality, with critics noting the band's growth and maturity since their previous releases. The album has also sparked conversations about mental health awareness, further solidifying Twenty One Pilots' role as influential voices in contemporary music.

Fans of the band have embraced "Breach" as a powerful addition to Twenty One Pilots' discography, appreciating the emotional depth and musical innovation that the duo continues to bring to their work. The album's release has been accompanied by a series of live performances and tours, allowing fans to experience the music in an immersive setting.
